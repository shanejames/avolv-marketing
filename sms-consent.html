SMS Consent & Terms

Last updated: March 2026

About Our SMS Messaging

Avolv.ai is an AI-powered business automation platform that enables small and medium businesses to communicate with their customers via SMS text messaging. Messages sent through our platform include:

  • Appointment reminders and confirmations
  • Service job notifications and updates
  • Estimate and invoice delivery
  • Post-service follow-up messages
  • Customer satisfaction surveys
  • Marketing promotions (for opted-in customers only)

How Consent Is Obtained

Customers provide consent to receive SMS messages through the following methods:

Online Opt-In

When creating an account, booking an appointment, or requesting a service through a business's website or application powered by Avolv.ai, customers provide their phone number and check a consent box agreeing to receive text message communications.

Verbal Consent

Customers may provide verbal consent when scheduling appointments or requesting services over the phone with a business. Verbal consent is logged with a timestamp in the Avolv.ai platform.

Keyword Opt-In

Customers can text START or SUBSCRIBE to a business's phone number to opt in to receiving text messages.

All consent is recorded with a timestamp in the Avolv.ai platform for compliance purposes.

Message Frequency

Message frequency varies based on the type of communication and the services being provided. Customers typically receive 1-10 messages per month depending on their service schedule and communication preferences. Marketing messages are limited to no more than 4 per month.

Message & Data Rates

Standard message and data rates may apply depending on your mobile carrier and plan. Avolv.ai does not charge for SMS messages, but your carrier may.

How to Opt Out

To stop receiving text messages at any time, simply reply:

STOP

to any message you receive from us.

After texting STOP, you will receive a single confirmation message and no further messages will be sent. You may opt back in at any time by texting START.
